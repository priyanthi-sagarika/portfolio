Strengths & Weakness

As a teacher with nearly 30 years of experience, I have honed my ability to create a positive and inclusive learning environment while overcoming numerous challenges within the education system. My strengths lie in fostering student engagement, tailoring lessons to diverse needs, and building strong student-teacher relationships.

At the same time, I have faced structural and systemic limitations that often restrict flexibility and innovation in teaching. These experiences have shaped my perspective, highlighting both the rewarding and challenging aspects of the teaching profession.

Strengths

Ability to create a positive and inclusive classroom environment. I firmly believe that every student deserves to feel valued and respected, and I work hard to build a sense of community among my students.
I use a variety of strategies, such as collaborative learning, educational technology, group assignments, different types of assessments, and student-led discussions, to encourage all my students to participate and contribute to my lessons.
Acknowledge and celebrate the diversity of backgrounds and experiences my students bring to the classroom. By creating a welcoming, empathetic, and supportive atmosphere, I feel as a teacher, I can help my students become more confident and motivated to learn.
Differentiate instruction to meet the needs of all learners. Most of the time, my classrooms consist of mixed-ability groups, and I firmly believe that every student has the potential to learn and succeed, even though they all have different learning styles and needs.

Therefore, I always assess the abilities, needs, and levels of my students to create lessons tailored to their learning styles and interests.
I also provide students with different opportunities to apply what they have learned in real-life situations. This approach has helped me create a classroom environment where every student feels supported and engaged in their learning enthusiastically.
I want to create a classroom where students can flourish and explore to reach their full potential. My goal is also to create a warm, loving environment, so students feel safe to take risks and express themselves.

Weakness

I often have to work within strict guidelines set by my school. These rules can restrict my freedom to make decisions about how to teach. I have faced some challenging experiences incorporating group work and pair work in my classroom, where I have to create learning experiences with minimal facilities available.
In the Sri Lankan context, there are significant disparities in the resources provided to schools. Many schools lack basic facilities, teaching materials, technological devices, and trained staff to employ modernized and engaging teaching styles. This hinders the quality of education that students receive and perpetuates cycles of disadvantage within communities.
Fixed curricula that teachers must follow, traditional textbooks written by inexperienced authors, a lack of facilities, administrative requirements, and education policies can limit teachers' flexibility to address diverse learning styles, varying needs, and differing abilities and backgrounds of students that require a personalized approach. All these challenges significantly affect how effective teachers can be in their roles. The implication is that for teachers to perform well, they need more support and freedom.
