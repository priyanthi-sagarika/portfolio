Short Course on Teaching English for
Specific Academic Purposes (ESAP)

I was selected to follow the Teaching English for Specific Academic Purposes (ESAP) course conducted by the Department of Languages, University of Moratuwa. This program consisted of four comprehensive modules:

  • Key Concepts in Teaching English for Specific Academic Purposes
    • Key concepts in ESAP
    • Conducting a needs analysis for ESAP
    • Communicative Language Teaching
    • Task-Based Language Teaching
    • Assessment in English Language Teaching and test specifications
  • Academic Reading and Writing
    • Genres of academic writing
    • Searching for academic resources
    • Organizing academic writing
  • Pedagogical Practices and Methodology in ESAP
    • Teacher roles in an adult ESAP classroom
    • Classroom management strategies
    • Teaching the four skills (listening, speaking, reading, and writing) to adults
    • Teaching grammar and vocabulary for academic purposes
  • Teaching Practicum

    As part of this module, I had the valuable opportunity to teach students at the University of Moratuwa. This hands-on experience allowed me to apply the theoretical knowledge gained during the course in a real academic environment, refine my teaching techniques, and interact with students in a university setting.

The Teaching English for Specific Academic Purposes course was an enriching experience that profoundly impacted my professional growth. The program equipped me with the skills to conduct effective needs analyses, design lessons tailored to adult learners, and employ innovative teaching methodologies such as task-based and communicative language teaching.

The practicum was particularly transformative, as it provided a platform to implement strategies for teaching grammar, vocabulary, and the four language skills in a structured and supportive academic environment. Working with university students enabled me to understand their unique challenges and aspirations, which has helped me become more empathetic and resourceful as an educator.

This experience reinforced my belief in the importance of adaptability, continuous learning, and fostering an engaging classroom atmosphere to meet the diverse needs of adult learners in academic settings. I was appointed to a post of visiting instructor in English in the Department of Languages, University of Moratuwa, Sri Lanka for the academic year 2018. This was one of the turning points in my journey of lifelong education.
