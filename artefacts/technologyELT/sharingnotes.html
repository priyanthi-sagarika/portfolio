Reflection on Using WhatsApp
as a Technological Tool

Initially, I felt unsure about how effective WhatsApp could be as a teaching tool. While I was familiar with its basic functions, using it for educational purposes was new territory. Managing group chats, ensuring students participated actively, and sharing multimedia content like documents and videos were initially overwhelming. I also felt frustrated when some students struggled with limited data or older devices that couldn’t support all features. However, I appreciated the platform’s accessibility and simplicity, which made it easier for most students to stay connected.

Using WhatsApp had both positive and negative aspects. On the positive side, it was widely accessible, even to students in remote areas with minimal internet connectivity. The ability to send recorded voice notes, videos, and images allowed me to deliver lessons in a flexible manner. It also created a direct line of communication with students and their families, fostering a stronger sense of support. On the negative side, the lack of real-time interaction made it challenging to ensure active engagement and immediate feedback. Managing multiple class groups and keeping track of responses was time-consuming and sometimes chaotic.

The experience underscored the importance of adaptability and simplicity in teaching methods. WhatsApp proved to be a powerful tool for reaching students who couldn’t join live sessions on platforms like Zoom. I learned to structure my lessons into manageable portions, using concise voice notes and step-by-step instructions to guide students. Additionally, I created assignments that could be completed offline and submitted via the app. Despite its limitations, WhatsApp became an effective bridge during a time when more advanced tools were not always feasible.

While using WhatsApp presented challenges, it also offered me a unique opportunity to develop creative solutions and foster stronger connections with my students. It taught me the importance of clear communication, patience, and flexibility in addressing the diverse needs of my learners.

I hope to continue incorporating WhatsApp as a supplementary tool, particularly for sharing updates, providing quick feedback, and maintaining communication with students and parents. I will also focus on creating more structured guidelines for its use to ensure better organization and clarity. Additionally, I will advocate for increased access to affordable internet and devices, ensuring that all students can benefit from such simple yet effective tools.
