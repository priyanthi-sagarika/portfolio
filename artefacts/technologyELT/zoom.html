Reflection on Zoom as a Technological Tool

At the beginning, I felt intimidated by the technical aspects of using Zoom. Managing features such as screen sharing, breakout rooms, and chat functions felt overwhelming at first. I also felt frustrated when some students could not join due to a lack of devices or poor internet connectivity. Despite these challenges, I appreciated the platform’s potential to bring us together during such a disruptive time, and I felt proud when I started mastering its features.

Using Zoom had both positive and negative aspects. On the positive side, it allowed real-time interaction, enabling me to maintain a connection with my students. Features like screen sharing and annotation helped make lessons more dynamic. On the negative side, poor internet connections disrupted lessons frequently, and some students were left out entirely due to lack of access. Keeping students engaged was another challenge, as the virtual environment often felt impersonal.

The experience highlighted the importance of understanding and utilizing digital tools effectively. I realized that Zoom could be a powerful teaching platform when used creatively, but it required significant preparation. For example, I started using polls and breakout rooms to encourage participation, which improved engagement. However, the inequity in access to devices and stable internet was a major barrier, showing the digital divide’s impact on education.

While teaching via Zoom was challenging, it was also an opportunity for growth. I became more confident in using technology and learned strategies to make online lessons more interactive. However, the experience also underscored the importance of addressing access issues to ensure no student is left behind.

In the future, I plan to further explore Zoom’s features to enhance engagement, such as using virtual whiteboards and integrating third-party tools for collaboration. I will also ensure lessons are recorded and shared for students who cannot join live sessions. Additionally, I will advocate for policies that improve access to devices and internet for all students, making digital learning more inclusive.
